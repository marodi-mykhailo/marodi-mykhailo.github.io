SYMPTOMS

There are four main symptoms:

  1. Depressive feeling disorder. Joy, deep "life" longing, internal anxiety and fear, irritability, a sense of emptiness, despair, guilt, hopelessness, a sense of apathy and dullness.
  2. Thinking disorders. General numbness, depressed thinking, inability to make decisions, problems with concentration and memory.
  3. Disorders of motor skills. Hypodynamic, depressive numbness, slowdown, facial expressions, lowered arms. Or: noticeable anxiety, thirst or confusion.
  4. Somatic / vegetative disorders.

Depression episode criteria
(according to DSM V):

  1. Five or more of the following symptoms occurred for two weeks or more, at least one of which was depressed mood or anhedonia:
    1. Depressed mood for most of the day and almost every day.
    2. Clearly less interest and satisfaction with almost all activities.
    3. Weight loss despite the lack of diets or weight gain or a decrease or increase in appetite present almost every day.
    4. Insomnia or increased drowsiness almost every day.
    5. Psychomotor agitation or slowdown occurring almost every day.
    6. Fatigue or loss of energy present almost every day.
    7. Self-esteem or excessive, inadequate guilt present almost every day.
    8. Reduced ability to concentrate or lack of decision making present almost every day.
    9. Recurrent thoughts of death, recurrent suicidal thoughts without an explicit suicide plan or with a suicide plan or attempted suicide.
  2. Symptoms are the cause of clinically significant suffering or impairment in social, professional or other important areas of functioning.
  3. There is no reason to associate this episode to the action of substances taken by the patient or other medical reasons related to the patient's state of health.
  4. The occurrence of these symptoms is not easier to explain by the presence of schizoaffective disorder, schizophrenia, schizophrenia-like disorder, delusional disorder or other psychotic disorder spectrum disorders.
  5. There has never been an episode of mania or hypomania.
